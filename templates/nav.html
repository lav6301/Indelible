THE CONSTITION OF INDIA

.

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the country, adopted on January 26, 1950. It serves as the framework for the political system, defines the rights and duties of citizens, and establishes the structure of the government. The purpose is to ensure justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity. It outlines the powers and functions of different branches of government and provides a comprehensive set of principles guiding the nation. The Constitution acts as a bulwark for protecting individual freedoms and fostering a democratic and inclusive society.


We th people of india,having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a sovereign democratic republicand to secure to all its citizens:Justice, social,economic and political; Equalityof status and opportunity:and to promote among them allows Fraternityassuring the dignity of the individual and the unity of the nation In our constituent assembly this twenty-sixth day of november,1949,dohereby adopt,enact and give to ourselves this Constitution.
